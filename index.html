Lake Tanganyika: Past and Present
I returned to Lake Tanganyika after six years and all I got were wet feet
The Lodge
The Tanganyika Science Lodge is located east of Kasakalawe, a town west of the northern Zambian town Mpulungu at the south of Lake Tanganyika, in the south-east of central Africa. Water levels of the lake have risen 2.5 meters in four years with no sign of stopping - this forces the inhabitants to reshape their living arrangements on the shore, documented here in some examples.
The People
Celestine, manager of the lodge, and her extended family, are still around. But since the lake has claimed some 15 meters of shoreline, new paths have formed. Also, the lake has regurgitated a big green metal anchor. It's too big to move, and also pretty cool.
The Work
Shafty's old pickup truck is, believe it or not, still running. But he doesn't want to drive to the lodge anymore, as the road would probably kill it. Luckily he doesn't have to because fuel is now stored in town.
The Interactions
Some places are under water completely, one being the so-called Heli-Pad. The kids are now older but would probably still be impressed by a drone.

The Huts
Four or five huts are now unusable or completely gone with the waves. Others have been built, but the slope of the shore is increasing, making it more difficult should the water rise any more.
